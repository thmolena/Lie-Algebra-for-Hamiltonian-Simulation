Hamiltonian Simulation · Lie-Algebra Methods · Operator Learning

Provable operator learning of size-transferable Trotter corrections.

The finite-step defect of a product formula is the unique unitary residual $R_q=U\,S_q^\dagger$; its generator $K_q=i\log R_q$ is geometrically local for the TFIM and is learned by one translation-equivariant network that transfers from 4–5-qubit chains to 10-qubit chains under a closed-form $r\eta$ bound.

Product-formula (Trotter–Suzuki) error is recast as an operator to be learned under provable guarantees: the residual factor is the unique norm-optimal left correction, Pauli-subspace projection is the unique Frobenius-optimal compression, and approximate residuals convert per-step error into global error linearly. Every number here is recomputed from deterministic dense-matrix simulation. This page is a condensed brief; the full project report carries the complete theory, statistics, and an interactive demo.

Background & Method

The finite-step defect as a structured Lie-algebra operator.

Digital Hamiltonian simulation realizes $U(t)=\exp(-iHt)$ by composing factors of a splitting $H=A+B$. For the order-$q$ step $S_q(\delta t)$, the residual $R_q=US_q^\dagger$ is the unique unitary left correction, and its Hermitian generator $K_q=i\log R_q$ lives in the dynamical Lie algebra. The benchmark Hamiltonian is the open-boundary TFIM:

$$ H = A + B, \qquad A = J\sum_{j=1}^{n-1} Z_j Z_{j+1}, \qquad B = h\sum_{j=1}^{n} X_j. $$

Generator structure & locality

For Strang ($q=2$), $K_2(\delta t)=\delta t^3 K_2^{(3)}+O(\delta t^5)$ with $K_2^{(3)}$ built from $[A,[A,B]]$ and $[B,[A,B]]$. For the open-boundary TFIM these double commutators have Pauli weight $\le 3$ — a proven locality theorem.

Pauli-weight compressibility

Most of the generator's Frobenius mass is low-weight: at $n=5$, $q=2$, $\delta t=0.1$, weight $\le 3$ holds $99.997\%$ of the squared-coefficient mass. Pauli projection $\Pi_w K_q$ is the unique Frobenius-optimal compressed generator.

Learned residual model

A translation-equivariant MLP (12 inputs → two width-64 tanh layers → 48 weight-$\le3$ coefficients, weights shared across sites and chain lengths) predicts $\delta t^{-3}$-scaled coefficients from local couplings — never the dense $2^n$ propagator.

Contributions & Novelty

What is new, stated precisely.

  • Exact factorization & optimality. $R_q=US_q^\dagger$ is the unique unitary left correction and a global minimizer of one-step error in every unitarily invariant norm.
  • Multi-step stability. Approximate residuals give global error $\le r\eta$ (and $\le r\lVert K_q-\widehat K_q\rVert_2$); the linear-in-$r$ rate is proven order-optimal.
  • Frobenius-optimal compression + TFIM locality. Pauli projection is the unique optimal compressed generator; the leading Strang generator has weight $\le3$ for the TFIM.
  • Learned residual mode (central advance). One network transfers from $n=4,5$ to $n\le10$ ($R^2=0.9998$), cuts the Strang error by $\sim\!38\text{--}46\times$, trains without a dense $2^n$ oracle from local patches, and surpasses leading-order BCH by >10× where it fails — all under the $r\eta$ bound.
  • Reproducibility. Deterministic dense-matrix experiments, figures, tables, and code recompute every number from first principles.

Finite-precision caveat

The exact residual theorem is an algebraic identity; in floating point the $q=8$ baseline at $\delta t=0.1$ is already near round-off, so the oracle residual can match or slightly exceed it. Values near $10^{-13}$–$10^{-14}$ are the double-precision floor, not physical error. Compression results are judged well above round-off (e.g. the $w=3,4$ rows).

Conclusion & Impact

An oracle target, made safe to approximate — and learned without the oracle.

Meaning

Compilation becomes a learning problem with a proof attached: a single per-site network trained on small systems from oracle-free patches corrects systems $32\times$ larger in Hilbert-space dimension, surpasses the analytic leading-order correction where it fails, and stays inside a closed-form $r\eta$ error budget.

Transparent limitations

Oracle RGTC needs $U(\delta t)$ and is as hard as exact dense simulation. The oracle-free learned residual removes the dense $2^n$ oracle from training, but evaluation here is still dense (up to ten qubits), templates are TFIM-specific and leading-order, results use double precision, and native-gate synthesis is not attempted. These define the next tasks; they do not invalidate the contribution.
