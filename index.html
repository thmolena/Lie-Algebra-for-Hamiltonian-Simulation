Lie-algebraic spectral truncation of the product-formula residual

The finite-step error of a Trotter–Suzuki formula is a single Hermitian operator; compressing it by spectral truncation yields a certified, geometrically convergent, learnable correction that enlarges the error–cost frontier of Hamiltonian simulation.

For an order-$q$ step $S_q(\delta t)$, the residual factor $R_q=U(\delta t)S_q(\delta t)^{\dagger}$ is the unique unitary left correction with $R_qS_q=U(\delta t)$, and its Hermitian generator $K_q=i\log R_q$ admits an exact, optimal correction. Projecting $K_q$ onto the Pauli-weight filtration of the Lie algebra of Hermitian operators is a genuine spectral truncation of that algebra—the Hamiltonian-simulation counterpart of the operator-algebraic spectral truncations of C$^\ast$-algebraic kernel learning, but endowed with a quantitative convergence rate, an a priori dynamical certificate, and a symmetric gate-level compilation that construction does not provide.

The most novel contribution

A spectral truncation with a rate, a certificate, and a repair

Recasting Pauli-weight projection of the residual generator as a spectral truncation of the Lie algebra of Hermitian operators places this work alongside the operator-algebraic spectral truncations recently introduced in kernel learning (Hashimoto et al., Spectral Truncation Kernels, 2024): both contract a noncommutative algebra to a tractable, structure-respecting subspace through one interpolation parameter. The simulation setting, however, controls a dynamics, and so supplies three guarantees the learning construction does not.

A convergence rate

The truncated-correction error decays geometrically in the level—provably at least $\delta t^{-1}$ per level, and measured at $449\times$ ($q=2$) and $576\times$ ($q=4$) per level on the transverse-field Ising chain—rather than merely converging in a limit. A rate is what makes the truncation level a usable accuracy dial.

A dynamical certificate

Every level carries the a priori bound $\lVert\widehat G_{q,w}^{\,r}-U(t)\rVert_2\le r\lVert K_q-\Pi_wK_q\rVert_2$, computable from $K_q$ without simulating the dynamics. The minimal bandwidth for a target accuracy $\varepsilon$ is therefore read off before any simulation, and grows only logarithmically in $1/\varepsilon$.

A symmetric repair

The naive first-order inner compilation of the truncated correction has an $O(\delta t^{2(q+1)})$ error floor that fails at low base order; a symmetric inner compilation lowers it to $O(\delta t^{3(q+1)})$ and recovers the truncated-oracle accuracy—the simulation analogue of the positivity-restoring smoothing the kernel construction requires.

Downstream payoff

The truncation is not an end in itself. A spectrally-truncated fourth-order correction reaches global spectral-norm errors lying between standard sixth- and eighth-order Suzuki formulas at $4.2$–$5.2\times$ fewer two-qubit gates than the cheapest standard formula of matching accuracy, placing new Pareto-optimal points on the product-formula error–cost frontier. The kernel construction has no analogue of such a measurable, gate-level payoff.

The guarantees, stated precisely

Theorem — geometric convergence rate

The spectral truncation converges geometrically in the level.

Reading. Each excluded weight level carries residual content of strictly higher order in $\delta t$; the measured per-level factor ($449$–$576\times$) is steeper still, from the symmetric cancellations that also lower the effective locality threshold.

Corollary — certificate-driven bandwidth

The truncation level is selected from the target accuracy alone.

The tails $\tau_0\ge\tau_1\ge\cdots\ge\tau_n=0$ are computable from $K_q$ without time evolution, and $w^\star(\varepsilon)=\min\{w:r\tau_w\le\varepsilon\}$ guarantees $\lVert\widehat G_{q,w^\star}^{\,r}-U(t)\rVert_2\le\varepsilon$. Under the geometric rate, $w^\star(\varepsilon)=O(\log(1/\varepsilon))$.

Reading. An a priori accuracy-to-parameter map, in place of a purely structural guarantee.

Theorem — symmetric faithful compilation

A symmetric inner product formula repairs the compilation floor.

The palindromic product $\widehat R_q^{\mathrm{s}}=\big(\prod_i e^{-iL_i/2}\big)\big(\prod_i e^{-iL_i/2}\big)^{\!\leftarrow}$ over the commuting layers obeys $\lVert\widehat R_q^{\mathrm{s}}-e^{-i\widehat K_q}\rVert_2\le c\,\Lambda^3$ with $\Lambda=O(\delta t^{q+1})$, an inner error of $O(\delta t^{3(q+1)})$—one order of $(q{+}1)$ below the first-order bound.

Reading. The structural defect of the naive realization is removed by symmetry, the simulation analogue of the kernel construction's positivity-restoring smoothing.
